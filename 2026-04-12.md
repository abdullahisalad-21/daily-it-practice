# 📚 What I Learned
- Hash functions are deterministic, one‑way, and sensitive to tiny changes  
- MD5 and SHA‑1 are broken and should not be used for security  
- X.509 certificates rely on extensions, trust chains, and revocation mechanisms  
- PGP was created to protect civil liberties and privacy  
- L2TP requires IPsec for encryption, while OpenVPN uses TLS  
- Physical access can bypass even strong cryptography if implementation is weak
